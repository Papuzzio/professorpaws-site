🐾Professor Paws

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 18, 2026

Professor Paws is an educational application that helps children build homework and learning skills with the support of an AI coach. Because the application is intended for children, protecting their personal information is central to how it is designed and operated. This Privacy Policy explains what information is collected, how it is used and safeguarded, and the rights available to parents and guardians.

This policy applies to the Professor Paws mobile application and related services (collectively, the "Service"), operated by Phillip Apuzzio ("Professor Paws," "we," "us," or "our"). By creating an account, the parent or guardian agrees to the practices described here.

Summary for parents

We know you are careful about your child's information, so here is the short version:

  • A parent or guardian creates the account and grants permission before a child can use the Service.
  • We collect only the information needed to help your child learn and to show you their progress.
  • The coaching is provided by an artificial intelligence system. Your child interacts with an AI, not a human teacher.
  • We do not sell personal information, do not display third-party advertising, and do not use your child's information to train AI models.
  • You may review, export, or delete your child's information, or withdraw consent, at any time by emailing professorpaws.app@gmail.com.

The full terms follow.

1. Scope and who we are

The Service is operated by Phillip Apuzzio, located in the United States. This policy governs personal information collected through the Service, available at playprofessorpaws.com. Questions about this policy or your child's information may be directed to professorpaws.app@gmail.com.

2. Children and parental control

The Service is designed for children of approximately 8 to 13 years of age and is intended to be established and supervised by a parent or legal guardian.

A parent or guardian must create the account and provide verifiable consent before any child uses the Service. Children do not create their own accounts and cannot make purchases; all purchases are made by the account-holding parent or guardian. The Service is not directed to adults seeking a service for their own use.

3. An AI-powered service

The instruction, explanations, hints, and feedback provided through the Service are generated by an artificial intelligence system (the Claude models provided by Anthropic, PBC). Your child is interacting with an AI system rather than a human. While the AI is designed to teach and encourage, it may occasionally produce incorrect information and is not a substitute for a teacher, tutor, or professional educational evaluation.

4. Information we collect

We collect the following categories of information.

Account information provided by the parent or guardian.

Name, email address, and password; subscription status and transaction confirmations; and communications you send us, such as support requests. Payments are processed by Apple through in-app purchase, and we do not receive or store full payment card numbers.

Child information provided or generated through use.

The child's first name or nickname, age or grade level, and any learning needs the parent chooses to share; homework content the child uploads or enters, which may include photographs of homework and typed problems and answers and can contain the child's name, handwriting, or other details; and learning activity, including problems attempted, answers given, hints used, skills practiced, progress, and in-app rewards earned and items selected.

Information collected automatically.

Limited technical and diagnostic information necessary for the Service to function and remain secure, such as application version and error data. We do not use third-party advertising or analytics technologies, and we do not collect precise geolocation. We do not record or access your child's voice, and the application does not use the device microphone; the read-aloud feature is one-directional, and a child's responses are typed.

5. How we use information

We use personal information solely to operate and improve the core Service: to generate personalized, paced homework guidance and read instructions aloud; to track a child's progress and provide parents with an accurate skill overview; to operate the companion and rewards features; to manage accounts, process subscriptions, and provide support; and to maintain the security of the Service and comply with legal obligations.

6. What we never do

We do not sell or rent personal information. We do not share personal information for third-party advertising or behavioral targeting. We do not use a child's information, including homework content, to train AI models; our AI provider processes this content only to generate responses and does not train its models on it under our commercial agreement. We do not permit children to make purchases, navigate outside the application, or exchange information with other users.

7. Verifiable parental consent

Because the Service is directed to children, we obtain verifiable parental consent before collecting personal information from a child. A parent or guardian must create and control the account and affirmatively provide consent during setup. For paid subscriptions, consent is further confirmed through the parent's verified payment transaction completed with Apple.

A parent may withdraw consent at any time by emailing professorpaws.app@gmail.com or deleting the account. Upon withdrawal, we will cease collecting the child's information and delete it as described in Section 11.

8. Legal bases for processing (EEA and United Kingdom)

Where the EU or UK General Data Protection Regulation applies, we process personal data on the following bases: the consent of the holder of parental responsibility for processing a child's data through the Service; the performance of our contract with the account holder to provide the Service; and our legitimate interests and legal obligations relating to security, fraud prevention, and compliance, balanced against the rights of the child. The age of digital consent varies between 13 and 16 across member states; the Service requires parental consent for all child users and therefore satisfies the strictest applicable threshold.

9. Service providers and disclosures

We share personal information only with service providers that help us operate the Service, under agreements requiring them to safeguard the information and process it solely on our instructions:

  • Anthropic, PBC provides the AI that generates educational content. Personal data is processed in the United States under a Data Processing Addendum incorporating Standard Contractual Clauses and the UK Addendum. Anthropic does not train its models on our data, and API content is deleted on a short retention schedule.
  • Supabase provides database, authentication, and file storage services, under a Data Processing Addendum incorporating Standard Contractual Clauses and the UK Addendum.
  • Apple processes subscription payments through in-app purchase, governed by Apple's own terms and privacy policy.

We may also disclose information where required by law, to protect the safety of a child or others, or in connection with a business transfer, in which case a child's information would remain subject to protections at least as protective as those in this policy.

10. International data transfers

Our service providers are located in the United States, and personal information is processed there. Where personal data is transferred from the EEA or United Kingdom, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses and the UK International Data Transfer Addendum to provide an adequate level of protection.

11. Data retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to provide the Service and fulfill the purposes described in this policy. Account and child profile information is retained while the account is active and deleted within thirty days of account deletion. Homework content is retained only as long as needed to generate guidance and support progress tracking and is deleted within thirty days. Learning activity is retained while the account is active and deleted within thirty days of account deletion. AI provider content is deleted by Anthropic on its short retention schedule. When an account or a child's data is deleted, the information is removed from active systems and from backups in the ordinary backup cycle.

12. How we protect information

We maintain technical and organizational safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of children's information, including encryption of data in transit and at rest; strict access controls and per-account data isolation, so that one family's information is never accessible to another; a written information-security program that limits access to children's information; and rate limiting and monitoring to detect and prevent misuse. No system can be guaranteed perfectly secure; in the event of a data breach, we will notify affected users and the relevant authorities as required by law.

13. Parental rights and choices

At any time, a parent or guardian may review the personal information held about their child; correct or update it; delete it or close the account entirely; obtain a portable copy; withdraw consent and stop further collection; and, in the EEA and United Kingdom, object to or restrict certain processing. To exercise these rights, email professorpaws.app@gmail.com. We will respond within the time required by applicable law.

Parents and guardians in the EEA and United Kingdom also have the right to lodge a complaint with their local data protection authority.

14. Children in the EEA and United Kingdom

We design the Service with the best interests of the child as a primary consideration, consistent with the United Kingdom Age Appropriate Design Code and applicable EU data protection law. This includes high-privacy defaults, data minimization, no profiling for marketing purposes, geolocation disabled, plain-language explanations, and a clear indication to the child when a parent is able to view their activity.

15. United States — Children's Online Privacy Protection Act

The Service complies with the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). We provide notice to parents, obtain verifiable parental consent before collecting personal information from children under 13, collect only information that is reasonably necessary, and afford parents the right to review and delete their child's information and to decline further collection. To exercise these rights, email professorpaws.app@gmail.com.

16. Changes to this policy

If we make a material change to how we handle children's information, we will notify the account holder by email or within the application and, where required by law, obtain renewed parental consent before the change takes effect.
